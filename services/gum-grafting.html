What is Gum Grafting?

Gum grafting is a periodontal surgical procedure that restores receded gum tissue to cover exposed tooth roots. It protects teeth from sensitivity and decay, reduces further recession, and improves smile symmetry.

Benefits

  • Protects exposed roots and reduces sensitivity
  • Prevents further gum recession
  • Improves smile aesthetics and symmetry
  • Supports long-term periodontal health

The Procedure

1

Consultation & Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of gum recession and contributing factors with personalized treatment planning.

2

Graft Selection

Selection of connective tissue graft, free gingival graft, or donor material based on your clinical needs.

3

Graft Placement

Precise placement and stabilization of the graft to cover exposed roots and thicken gum tissue.

4

Healing & Follow‑up

Regular monitoring and gentle care recommendations to ensure optimal healing and long-term stability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I a candidate for gum grafting?

Candidates include patients with gum recession, root sensitivity, or thin tissue. A clinical evaluation will determine the ideal grafting approach for your case.

Will the results look natural?

Yes. With precise techniques and careful tissue handling, the final appearance blends seamlessly with adjacent gums.

What is the recovery time?

Initial healing typically occurs within 2–3 weeks. We provide detailed post‑operative instructions for a smooth recovery.
