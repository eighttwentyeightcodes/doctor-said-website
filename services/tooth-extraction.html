About Tooth Extraction

While preserving natural teeth is always our priority, extractions may be necessary due to severe decay, infection, fracture, or orthodontic reasons. We prioritize patient comfort and atraumatic techniques to protect surrounding tissues.

Benefits

  • Gentle, atraumatic removal
  • Options for socket preservation
  • Immediate or early implant placement when appropriate
  • Clear aftercare for smooth recovery

Treatment Steps

1

Assessment & Planning

Clinical exam and imaging to plan a comfortable, efficient extraction and replacement strategy.

2

Atraumatic Extraction

Minimally invasive techniques to remove the tooth while preserving bone and soft tissue.

3

Socket Preservation (Optional)

Bone grafting placed to maintain the site for future implant placement.

4

Immediate Implant (When Indicated)

In selected cases, an implant can be placed during the same visit for faster recovery and fewer appointments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the extraction be painful?

We use effective local anesthesia and gentle techniques. Most patients report only mild discomfort after the procedure.

How long is recovery?

Initial healing typically takes 7–10 days. We provide tailored aftercare instructions to ensure a smooth recovery.

Can I replace the tooth immediately?

In many cases, yes—an immediate implant or temporary replacement may be possible depending on your clinical situation.
