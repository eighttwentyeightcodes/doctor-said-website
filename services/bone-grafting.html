Predictable Outcomes

What is Bone Grafting?

Bone grafting restores missing jawbone volume and density. It creates a stable foundation for dental implants and helps maintain natural facial contours after tooth loss.

Benefits

  • Enables dental implants where bone is insufficient
  • Preserves facial structure and prevents collapse
  • Improves long‑term implant success rates
  • Restores natural contours of the jaw

Common Procedures

1

Socket Preservation

Graft placed immediately after extraction to preserve bone for future implant placement.

2

Sinus Lift

Elevation of the sinus floor with graft material to allow implants in the upper back jaw.

3

Ridge Augmentation

Rebuilding thin or uneven ridges to achieve adequate bone width and height.

4

Guided Bone Regeneration

Membrane‑assisted grafting to encourage new bone growth in targeted areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bone grafts always succeed?

Success rates are high when case selection and technique are appropriate. We use proven materials and protocols to maximize outcomes.

When can an implant be placed after grafting?

Depending on the procedure and site, implants may be placed immediately or after 3–6 months of healing.

What materials are used for grafts?

We use biocompatible materials including autografts, allografts, xenografts, and synthetics, selected based on your specific needs.
